COUNCIL TAX – COMPLETE GUIDE (UK)

What is Council Tax?
Council Tax is a local tax collected by your local council to pay for services like:
- Waste collection
- Police and fire services
- Local schools and infrastructure

Who needs to pay?
- Adults (18+) living in a property
- Owners or tenants depending on the situation

Who is exempt?
- Full-time students
- People under 18
- Some disabled individuals
- People in care homes

---

PAYING COUNCIL TAX

To pay your council tax:

Step 1:
Find your local council using your postcode:
https://www.gov.uk/find-local-council

Step 2:
Go to your council’s website

Step 3:
Look for:
- "Pay Council Tax"
- "Council Tax account"

Step 4:
Choose payment method:
- Direct Debit
- Online payment
- Bank transfer

---

IF YOU DON'T HAVE AN ACCOUNT

You usually DO NOT create a new account manually.

Instead:
- Your council automatically creates your account when you move in
- You receive a Council Tax bill with your account number

If you don't have it:
👉 Contact your local council

---

STUDENT EXEMPTION

If you're a student:

Step 1:
Get student certificate from university

Step 2:
Apply here:
https://www.gov.uk/council-tax/discounts-for-full-time-students

Step 3:
Submit to your local council

 IF YOU DO NOT PAY COUNCIL TAX

If you miss a payment:
- The council sends a reminder notice
- You must pay within 7 days

If you miss again:
- You lose the right to pay monthly
- Full yearly bill becomes due

Further consequences:
- Court summons issued
- Extra legal costs added
- Bailiffs may collect the debt
- Money can be deducted from wages or benefits

Serious cases:
- Enforcement action
- Possible imprisonment (rare)

Council tax is a local tax collected by councils in the UK to pay for services like rubbish collection, police, and schools.

You must pay council tax if you are over 18 and own or rent a home.

Students may be exempt from council tax or receive discounts.

You can pay council tax online through your local council website, usually by direct debit.

If you miss payments, the council may send reminders and take legal action.
